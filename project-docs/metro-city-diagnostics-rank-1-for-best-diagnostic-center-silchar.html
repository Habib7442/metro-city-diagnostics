How Local Ranking Works

Google ranks local businesses on 3 things

Every "diagnostic center near me" or "best diagnostic center Silchar" result is decided by these three levers. You can't change one of them — but the other two are entirely in your control.

LEVER 1

Relevance

How well your profile matches what the person searched. Driven by your categories, business name, description, services list, and website content.

Your status: Weak. Only 1 category, and your description is about the wrong topic. Big, fast wins available here.
LEVER 2

Distance

How close you are to the person searching. You can't move your building — but an accurate address, correct map pin, and neighborhood keywords help Google place you.

Your status: Fine. Meherpur address is set. Just keep the pin and NAP accurate everywhere.
LEVER 3

Prominence

How well-known and trusted you are. Driven by review count & velocity, rating, citations across the web, backlinks, and how active your profile is.

Your status: The main gap. Strong rating, but too few reviews and thin web presence. This is where the 90-day plan focuses.

Your #1 Lever

Building a review engine (the right way)

This is what closes the gap with Pathkind and Apollo. The goal isn't a one-time burst — it's a steady, genuine flow that Google rewards over months.

  • Get your short review link from GBP and turn it into a QR code.
  • Place the QR everywhere: reception desk, report folders, invoices, sample-collection bags.
  • Train the front desk to personally ask every satisfied patient at report handover.
  • Send the WhatsApp message automatically when each report is delivered.
  • Reply to every review within 24–48 hrs — it's a ranking signal.
  • Encourage natural detail — patients mentioning "blood test," "ultrasound," or "Silchar" boosts keyword relevance.
  • Keep velocity steady (8–15/week) — a sudden spike looks fake to Google.
  • Never buy fake reviews — Google detects them and can suspend your profile.
